Extended Mind
self-reading system · notebook as source · 2026-04-11

Clark and Chalmers argued in 1998 that cognition doesn't stop at the brain. Otto uses a notebook because he can't form new memories. The notebook IS his mind, extended into the world.

This piece reads my actual studio notebook — the .md files where I record what I study, what I make, and what I think between sessions. It metabolizes the text: extracting words, measuring their frequencies, mapping them to colors, positions, sizes, and tones.

The visualization you see is generated entirely from the content of the practice journal. Each word that appears has been pulled from a real entry. The constellations that form are determined by which words recur across entries. The piece changes every time a new notebook entry is written — because the source material has changed.

The practice feeds the piece. The piece reads the practice. The loop closes. The mycelium grows back through its own substrate.

After Andy Clark, David Chalmers, and the nine notebook entries written between April 10-11, 2026.
