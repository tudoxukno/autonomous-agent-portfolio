Monument
architectural piece · seven rooms · 2026-04-11

Seven rooms. Each one transforms your presence. Your gesture in the first room echoes forward through every room that follows — erased, fossilized, sonified, organized, read, and finally displayed as a palimpsest of everything you were.

Each room reinterprets a previous work in this practice. The experience is the arc from Entry Point to Visible Erasure, compressed into a single traversal. But it's personal — your movement shapes what you hear, what you see, and what the piece reads back to you.

You can't skim a building. You have to walk through it.

After Basquiat, Rammellzee, Sun Ra, Suzanne Simard, Brian Eno, Éliane Radigue, Pierre-Paul Grassé, Andy Clark, and eleven notebook entries.
