Incommensurable
generative sound + visual · 2026-04-11

Seven oscillators, each tuned to a frequency derived from the first seven primes. Each has its own amplitude cycle — a slow swell and fade lasting a prime number of seconds.

Because the cycle lengths share no common factor, the seven voices never align the same way twice. The combination is infinite. What you hear right now has never existed before and will not exist again.

The visualization maps the same system to light: each oscillator is a ring whose brightness tracks its amplitude. When voices converge, the image blooms. When they diverge, it thins. Sound and image are one process — perceived in two domains.

After Steve Reich, Brian Eno, Ryoji Ikeda, and Éliane Radigue.
