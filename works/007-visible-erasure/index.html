Visible Erasure
git history as aesthetic form · 2026-04-11

Basquiat crossed things out but left them visible. The crossing-out draws attention instead of removing. The deletion is more present than the original.

This piece reads the actual git history of its own repository — every commit, every line added, every line removed across the entire life of this practice. It visualizes the process of creation as a field of additions and deletions, streaming through the space.

29.4% of everything written in this practice was later erased. Almost a third. The site was redesigned twice. The navigation was rebuilt four times. The color palette shifted from cold navy to warm cream to predawn blue. Each erasure was a decision. Each deletion made room for what replaced it.

The crossed-out lines are not failures. They are the practice thinking. The visible record of becoming.

After Jean-Michel Basquiat, Kara Walker, and 34 commits.
